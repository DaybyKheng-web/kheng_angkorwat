Siem Reap, Cambodia

The heart of Cambodia's ancient beauty

Siem Reap is one of the most well-known cities in Cambodia, and for good reason. It’s home to some of the world’s most famous ancient temples, like Angkor Wat, and offers a unique mix of culture, history, and modern fun. I love this city because it blends peaceful temple visits with lively streets, amazing food, and friendly people.

Places You Shouldn’t Miss - Top 4 Tourist Attractions

"Angkor Wat" by PerfectLazybones – Adobe Stock

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is one of the most famous places in Cambodia. It’s a big temple that was built a long time ago and means a lot to the people here. I went early in the morning to watch the sunrise, and it was so peaceful and beautiful. It’s a moment I won’t forget. Learn more on UNESCO

"Pubstreet" by SNEHIT PHOTO – Adobe Stock

Pub Street

If you want to see the fun side of Siem Reap, Pub Street is the place to go. At night, the lights shine, music plays, and the street gets full of food, people, and energy. It’s a busy and lively spot — great for grabbing a snack and enjoying the night. See what’s happening

"Bayon Temple" by Only Fabrizio – Adobe Stock

Bayon Temple

Bayon Temple is known for its big stone faces that seem to smile at you. It’s part of an old city called Angkor Thom. Walking around there feels special, like you're stepping into a piece of history. More about Bayon

"Banteay Srei Temple" by milosk50 – Adobe Stock

Banteay Srei

Banteay Srei is a small temple, but it’s really pretty. It’s made of pink stone and has beautiful carvings. It’s quiet and calm, and I liked taking my time there to look at all the details. Discover it

Quick Facts

  • Location: Northwestern Cambodia, in Siem Reap Province
  • Population: About 140,000 people
  • Geography: Flat plains near Tonlé Sap Lake
  • Weather: Hot and humid year-round
  • Language: Khmer
  • Fun Fact: Angkor Wat is the only temple featured on a national flag!
